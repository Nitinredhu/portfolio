03 / The Problem

Bellabeat is a smart wellness company for women. To grow, they needed a data-backed marketing direction. The core challenge: What unique angle can a wellness company take in a market already heavily saturated by giants like Fitbit and Apple Watch? I needed to analyze existing smart device users to find friction points and unmet needs.

04 / My Approach

I utilized a public FitBit dataset containing daily activity, sleep, and intensity logs. I ran the entire analysis in R Language using ggplot2.

Instead of just aggregating step counts, I wanted to test the industry's core assumptions. I ran a linear regression to see if taking more steps actually improved sleep quality, and mapped out daily patterns to see exactly when users were abandoning their wellness routines.

06 / Key Data Insights
Insight 01 — 16.5h Sedentary Time
The average user spends 989 minutes (approx. 16.5 hours) per day doing absolutely nothing. Merely tracking steps isn't enough for these users; they need aggressive, habit-breaking nudges.
Insight 02 — Steps ≠ Better Sleep
The most surprising finding: R-squared was only 0.07 between steps and sleep hours. Mathematically, taking more steps did NOT lead to better sleep. The general market assumption is flawed.
Insight 03 — The Sunday Slump
User activity peaks on Saturday but completely crashes to its lowest point on Sunday. This identifies Sunday morning as the most critical vulnerability in user consistency.
07 / Business Impact & Recommendation
Shift the marketing narrative entirely away from "Step Counting" to "Balance & Recovery". Re-allocate digital ad budgets to target users with aggressive re-engagement campaigns specifically on Sunday mornings, and market the Bellabeat Membership as the expert fix for the sedentary crisis.
