My Journey

I am in my final semester of B.Tech in Information Technology at JMIT, Radaur (2022–2026). I started with software development, but over time I became more interested in understanding the data behind applications.

Instead of just building features, I wanted to know who was using the product, why users were leaving, and what could be improved.

That is how I moved into data analytics. I started working with SQL to explore data, used Power BI to visualize patterns, and learned how to turn raw data into simple, clear insights.

Right now, I am finishing my degree while building real projects with real datasets — not just practicing, but actually trying to answer real questions: Why do customers leave? What drives sales? Does studying more actually help grades?

How I Actually Work

PostgreSQL / SQL
My first tool on any new dataset. I use it to explore, filter, join tables, and clean messy raw data before I even think about analysis. If the data’s not clean, the insights aren’t either.
Power BI & Excel
Where the insights become visible. I build interactive dashboards using DAX that let stakeholders actually explore data — not just stare at a static chart. Excel for quick stuff; Power BI for the big picture.
R & Python
For when the questions get more serious. Linear regression, correlation analysis, ggplot2 visualizations — for projects where I need to actually prove something, not just show a trend.
