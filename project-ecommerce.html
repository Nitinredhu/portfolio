03 / The Problem

The business was generating significant revenue (₹13.63M) but allocating marketing and inventory budgets blindly across dozens of product categories. Without visibility into which specific items were actually driving the volume, capital was being tied up in dead stock while high-velocity items risked stocking out.

04 / My Approach

The original Olist dataset was fragmented across multiple tables and contained Portuguese category names. I used PostgreSQL to clean, translate, and join the orders, order_items, and products tables into a single source of truth.

I then performed exploratory data analysis (EDA) using SQL aggregations to break down revenue by category and time period, eventually feeding the clean views into Power BI to build a dynamic executive dashboard.

06 / Key Data Insights
Insight 01 — Extreme Concentration
The top 3 categories (Home/Bath, Beauty, Sports) drive a massive 68% of total volume. The remaining 70+ categories contribute a fraction of the revenue but take up valuable warehouse space.
Insight 02 — A Maturing Market
Revenue hit a peak of ~₹1M/month in early 2018 and then stabilized. This flat post-peak trend indicates a maturing market, requiring a shift from growth-hacking to operational efficiency.
Insight 03 — Logistics are Solid
With a 96.2% successful delivery rate, fulfillment is not a bottleneck. I checked this early to rule out operational issues. The core opportunity lies entirely on the sales and inventory optimization side.
07 / Business Impact & Recommendation
Management should immediately halt blanket inventory spending and aggressively reallocate budget toward the Top 3 categories. By cutting dead stock in the lowest-performing segments, the company can free up an estimated 22% in working capital while ensuring zero stock-outs for high-demand items.
