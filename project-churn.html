03 / The Problem

A fictional telecommunications company was bleeding customers—facing a massive 26.54% churn rate that cost them around ₹139K every year. The leadership team knew people were leaving, but they were completely blind as to who and why. They needed data to figure out where to actually focus their retention efforts.

04 / My Approach

I started by pulling the raw data into PostgreSQL to clean up the mess (like dealing with missing values in the 'TotalCharges' column and fixing bad data types). Once the data was clean, I wrote SQL queries to aggregate churn rates across different demographics and account types.

After shaping the data, I connected it to Power BI to build an exploratory dashboard. This let me slice the data visually and find the actual behaviors driving people to cancel their service.

06 / Key Data Insights
Insight 01 — The Contract Trap
Month-to-month contracts are where almost all the churn happens. Customers locked into 1 or 2-year contracts have near-zero churn. This proves that lock-in is a massive retention lever.
Insight 02 — The 6-Month Window
If a customer survives their first 6 months, they are significantly less likely to leave. The highest attrition happens immediately after onboarding. If you're going to intervene, do it early.
Insight 03 — Electronic Checks = Risk
Electronic check users churn at a noticeably higher rate. This surprised me. It's likely not about the payment gateway itself, but rather a proxy metric for customers with lower overall service engagement.
Insight 04 — Price isn't the whole story
Higher monthly charges correlate with higher churn only for month-to-month customers. Contract holders pay similar amounts but stay. The core issue is commitment, not price sensitivity.
07 / Business Impact & Recommendation
Stop offering random discounts to everyone. Instead, target month-to-month users right before their 3rd month with an aggressive upgrade offer to a 1-year contract. Converting just 15% of this high-risk group would recover ~₹42,000 in retained annual revenue.
